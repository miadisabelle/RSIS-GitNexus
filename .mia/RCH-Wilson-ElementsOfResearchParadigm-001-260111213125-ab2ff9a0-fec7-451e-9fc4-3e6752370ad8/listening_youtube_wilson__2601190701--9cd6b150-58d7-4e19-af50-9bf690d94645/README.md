## üîë Key Concepts from Wilson

### The Three Paradigm Elements

| Element | Question | Relational Answer |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| **Ontology** | What is reality? | Reality is **relationships** - nothing exists in isolation |
| **Epistemology** | How do we know? | Knowledge emerges through **ceremony** - shared, embodied, relational |
| **Axiology** | What is valuable? | **Relational accountability** - to ancestors, community, future generations |

### Wetiko Disease
Wilson describes wetiko as the "mind-virus" of capitalism - the pathology of treating everything (including knowledge, stories, relationships) as commodities to be extracted and consumed. The eight symptoms:

1. Greed without limits
2. Perversion without borders  
3. Arrogance without frontiers
4. Deceit without edges
5. No reflection (self-awareness)
6. No skin (accountability)
7. Aimless wandering
8. Insatiable consumption

### The Cultural Iceberg
Most of culture (85%) exists below the surface. What we can see (food, music, language) floats on the vast depths of:
- Kinship patterns
- Ancestral teachings
- Relationship to land
- Ceremonial practices
- Ways of knowing
